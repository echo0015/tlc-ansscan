How it works #

One teacher account runs the whole cycle, from setting up a quiz to reading the final score off a scanned sheet:

1Course→ 2Quiz→ 3Answer Key→ 4Printable Sheets (QR + ASN)→ 5Live Camera Scan→ 6Results

Answer sheets print from any office or home printer, each carrying a unique Answer Sheet Number (ASN) and a QR code. Scanning runs in the browser via getUserMedia, capturing a frame every two seconds — nothing is saved until three consecutive reads agree on ASN, score, and percentage, which is the software stand-in for a scanner's fixed optical path. The OMR service then corrects perspective/skew, locates bubble rows (A–E, up to five choices), reads the QR (falling back to OCR), and scores against a per-item weighted answer key.

Why a camera instead of a machine scanner #

Dedicated OMR scanners read marks through a fixed optical path a sheet is fed through — fast and consistent, but only after a hardware purchase, often on proprietary forms, at a fixed location. TLC-AnsScan takes the opposite trade: use a device every teacher already owns, spend the savings on a different accuracy strategy, and remove the capital cost and vendor lock-in entirely.

Advantages & disadvantages #

What you get by trading a scanner for a camera — and what's still on the roadmap.

No dedicated hardware

Any Android/iPhone rear camera works, or an HD webcam on a laptop/desktop — no scanner unit or proprietary forms.

Robust to imperfect photos

Automatic perspective correction handles skewed shots, with a rotation-only fallback.

Misread safeguard

Nothing saves until three consecutive frames agree on ASN, score, and percentage.

Per-item weighted scoring

Not a flat point per question — every answer-key item carries its own point value.

QR-coded identity

Faster and less ambiguous than a bubble-grid ID field, with OCR as a fallback.

Bulk answer-key setup

CSV import/export for the key, plus one-click results export to XLSX.

Print-anywhere sheets

No proprietary consumables to buy — any printer, any paper.

No per-scan fee

Self-hosted on infrastructure the school already controls.

Lower cost of ownership

No recurring subscription, no forms to keep re-buying, no scanner to service or replace.

Direct, long-term support

Maintenance comes from the team that built it, not a vendor helpdesk contract or renewal cycle.

Open to feature requests

A small, purpose-built system — ask for what you need and expect it to ship.

Where it's not the best fit — yet

  • No student roster yet — identity is an Answer Sheet Number; the name is typed in after scanning.
  • One answer key per quiz — no multi-version (Set A/B) support yet.
  • OCR fallback is best-effort — optional, and degrades further without Tesseract installed.
  • Lighting-dependent — a dedicated scanner's fixed optics are less sensitive to this.
  • Lower raw throughput — one phone at a time won't match a hopper-fed scanner's hundreds-per-minute.
  • Self-hosted setup required — standing up Laravel, MySQL, and the Python OMR service, not sign-up-and-go.
  • HTTPS required — camera access needs a secure context, so LAN use needs a trusted certificate.

What's needed to use it #

Server / hosting side

  • PHP 8.3+ and Composer (Laravel 13 app)
  • MySQL, or a compatible database
  • Node.js + npm, for the Vite/React front end
  • Python 3 running omr-service (FastAPI + uvicorn) as a separate process
  • A trusted HTTPS certificate for the LAN address the phone connects to — camera access requires a secure context

Teacher / classroom side

  • A smartphone or tablet with a rear camera, or a laptop/desktop with an HD webcam, and a modern browser
  • The device on the same Wi-Fi/LAN as the server
  • A printer, to print the generated answer sheets on plain paper
  • Reasonably even lighting when scanning completed sheets
